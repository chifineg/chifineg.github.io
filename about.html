About

A researcher grounded in care

I am a biomedical engineer interested in translating engineering insight into healthcare technologies that preserve dignity, safety, and independence.

Biography

My developing research practice brings together biomedical engineering, intelligent systems, rehabilitation technology, and assistive robotics. I am motivated by the opportunity to design tools that respond to real clinical and home-care needs—particularly as populations age and care systems become more stretched.

Alongside a professional foundation in biomedical equipment, I am building a focused programme of research toward graduate study and long-term interdisciplinary collaboration.

Education

Bachelor of Engineering in Biomedical Engineering. Academic interests include medical technology design, rehabilitation, and intelligent health systems.

Professional experience

Biomedical Equipment Engineer (2017–2021) and Biomedical Equipment Technologist (2023–2026), with experience supporting safe, reliable healthcare technology.

Research vision

To develop accessible, human-centred technologies that help people retain agency while improving the work of clinicians and caregivers.

Career goals

To undertake Master's and doctoral research, contribute to internationally connected research teams, and ultimately establish a collaborative research group.
