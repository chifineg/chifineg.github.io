Robotic Nursing Bed with Speech Recognition

A prototype concept investigating voice interaction as a more accessible way to operate nursing-bed functions.

View Project Prototype

Background

For people with mobility limitations, routine bed adjustments can depend on another person or on interfaces that are difficult to use.

Objectives

Investigate speech recognition as an intuitive control pathway and identify practical safety requirements for a care-focused device.

System design

The concept combines a voice-input interface, control logic, adjustable bed mechanisms, and a safety-first operating model.

Lessons learned

Accessible interaction must be matched by dependable recognition, fail-safe controls, and testing with the people affected by the technology.

Future improvements

Future iterations could use robust multilingual speech models, physical override controls, sensor feedback, and formal usability evaluation.
